About This Course

Applied Machine Learning is not just about training models on clean datasets. In practice, ML systems operate inside complex, messy environments shaped by delayed feedback, data drift, and business constraints.

This course bridges the gap between algorithms and production. We begin with the "Classic Stack" (Linear Models, Trees) and move to the "Modern GenAI Stack" (LLMs, RAG, Agents). Unlike theoretical courses, we emphasize problem framing, system design, and failure analysis. We treat models not as mathematical artifacts, but as powerful components to be fine-tuned, chained, and deployed to survive real-world traffic.

üí° Note on Deep Learning & Math

This course prioritizes Deep Learning and LLMs as the core of modern ML engineering. We cover mathematical foundations to ensure a "white-box" understanding for debugging and optimization, focusing on applied engineering logic rather than theoretical derivation.

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Problem Formulation: Translate ambiguous business requirements into well-defined ML tasks
  • System Reliability & Failure Analysis: Understand why models fail in production
  • Modern GenAI Stack: Implement RAG systems, perform SFT, and design LLM Agents
  • Classic ML Proficiency: Master feature engineering, pipeline construction, and error analysis using scikit-learn
  • Engineering Rigor: Utilize AI coding assistants effectively to ship production-ready code

üß© Prerequisites

  • Programming: Proficiency in Python 3 with data manipulation (pandas, numpy, pytorch)
  • Mathematics: Linear Algebra (vector/matrix operations) and Statistics (probability, distributions)
  • Tooling: Familiarity with AI coding assistants is strongly recommended
  • Mindset: Willingness to adapt - the ML field changes weekly!

    üìú Course Policies

    Zoom Support

    Lectures will be streamed via Zoom and recorded on a "best effort" basis. Technical failures may result in the loss of both the live stream and the recording. Recommendation: We strongly recommend attending in-person to guarantee access to the material.

    Attendance

    Attendance is optional and not graded. We treat you as adults; you do not need to email the instructor to report or explain absences.

    Late Work

    Late submissions will be accepted up to 24 hours after the original deadline with a 10% penalty. Work submitted more than 24 hours past the deadline will not be accepted. Exceptions: We understand that life happens. Please submit a request via the Google Form (announced in Ed) before the deadline.

    Re-grading

    Regrade requests must be submitted in writing to the TAs within one week of the grade release. Note that requesting a regrade triggers a full review of your assignment.

    AI Policy

    Use of AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.) is fully permitted for all assignments. You are expected to verify AI-generated outputs. Code that crashes or logic that fails will be graded accordingly. While AI tools are permitted, plagiarism of other students' work is strictly prohibited. You are personally responsible for the accuracy and logic of your submissions.
